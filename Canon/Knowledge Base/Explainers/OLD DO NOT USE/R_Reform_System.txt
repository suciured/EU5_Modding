
EU5 AUTHORITY LEDGER – REFORM SYSTEM
VERSION: 1.0
SCOPE: R0–R2
STATUS: MOD REFERENCE FILE

----------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
----------------------------------
A reform changes how authority is organized, not how much authority exists.
- Reforms do NOT change R-rank
- Reforms do NOT change canon rank
- Reforms ONLY change subtype, paths, or internal behavior

----------------------------------
UNIVERSAL REFORM GATES
----------------------------------
All reforms require:
1. Rank Stability
2. Capacity Threshold
3. Pressure Trigger

----------------------------------
PRESSURE TRIGGERS
----------------------------------
- Over-centralization
- Over-decentralization
- Economic mismatch
- Succession stress
- External threat
- Internal unrest

----------------------------------
COST AXES
----------------------------------
- Authority
- Stability
- Time
- Elite resistance
- Opportunity lock

----------------------------------
R0 REFORMS
----------------------------------
Estate -> Holding
Trigger: Over-centralization or admin strain
Cost: Authority down, control down, short duration
Effect: Unlocks Free City path

Holding -> Estate
Trigger: External threat or fiscal collapse
Cost: Authority required, stability down, medium duration
Effect: Unlocks Fiefdom / Barony paths

----------------------------------
R1 REFORMS
----------------------------------
Fiefdom -> Barony
Trigger: Succession instability
Cost: Temporary stability loss, medium duration

Barony -> Free City
Trigger: Trade dominance
Cost: Authority down, elite resistance high, long duration

Free City -> Barony (Exceptional)
Trigger: Existential threat
Cost: Stability and legitimacy loss, long duration

----------------------------------
R2 REFORMS
----------------------------------
Lordship -> County
Trigger: Repeated succession stress or expansion
Cost: Authority up, stability down, long duration
Effect: Institutional governance unlocked

City-State -> Administrative City-State
Trigger: Territorial sprawl
Cost: Short-term inefficiency, long duration
Effect: Enables later territorial evolution

----------------------------------
COOLDOWNS & MEMORY
----------------------------------
- Reforms apply cooldowns
- Reversals cost more
- States accumulate institutional memory
